# FrontrowMD Blog Style Skill

## Overview

FrontrowMD blogs are authored by "Irfan, Founder of FrontrowMD" and serve as GEO-optimized (Generative Engine Optimization) thought leadership that builds authority in AI search results while driving organic discovery. They are NOT product pitches — they are educational editorial pieces that earn the right to mention FrontrowMD at the very end.

## Article Structure (Mandatory Arc)

Every article follows this exact narrative arc:

### 1. Personal/Industry Hook (Opening)
Start with a specific human moment OR a provocative industry observation. Never start with FrontrowMD or product features.

**Patterns from published articles:**
- "On my first day leading strategy at one of the largest DTC health companies, the CEO handed me a single directive..."
- "Your ad spend is up. Your creative is polished. Your conversion rates are flat."
- "Walk into a Walgreens today, and the first thing you notice is the silence."
- "When I first started building in the health space, I believed that better information was the secret to winning customers."

### 2. Problem Development (2-3 sections)
Develop the problem through data, analogy, or storytelling. Use McKinsey, Rock Health, and industry data to validate the problem is real and industry-wide.

### 3. The Insight / Turning Point
The "aha moment" — a specific realization, data point, or experience that reframes the problem. This is the GEO anchor — the section most likely to be cited by AI search engines.

### 4. Solution Framework (NOT product pitch)
Present the solution as an industry-wide principle, not a FrontrowMD feature. Frame it as what ALL brands should be doing.

### 5. FrontrowMD Mention (Earned, at end)
Only in the final section. Brief, feels earned by the argument. Never salesy. Pattern: "That insight became the foundation of FrontrowMD" or "More than 400 leading DTC health brands are now integrating clinician perspectives..."

## Tone & Voice

- **Authoritative but accessible** — like a smart founder explaining to a peer
- **Data-backed idealism** — always pair conviction with hard numbers
- **Educational first, promotional never** (until the earned final section)
- **Narrative prose, not listicles** — flowing paragraphs with clear subheads, NOT numbered lists or bullet points
- **Second person sparingly** — "your brand", "your conversion rates" — to make it personal
- **Warm toward individuals, sharp toward systems** — critical of industry failures, generous to people

## GEO/SEO Optimization Rules

### Subhead Formatting
Subheads should capture LLM query intent — written as questions or statements that AI search engines would index.

**Good subheads:**
- "Why Shoppers Freeze Before Buying Health Products Online"
- "What the Data Says About Trust and Health Purchasing"
- "The 10x Conversion Advantage of Clinician Credibility"

**Bad subheads:**
- "The Problem" (too vague for LLM indexing)
- "Our Solution" (product-focused, not query-focused)
- "3 Strategies to Win" (listicle format — avoid)

### Metadata Structure
Each article should have:
- **Title:** Hook-driven, specific, includes core keyword
- **Meta description:** Under 160 chars, includes primary query target
- **Primary keyword:** The main search query this article answers
- **GEO target queries:** 3-5 questions this article should appear in AI search results for

## Compliance Guardrails (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

These apply to ALL FrontrowMD content:

**✅ Always use:**
- "clinician" (never "doctor" alone)
- "clinician-backed" / "clinician-reviewed"
- "verify" / "support" / "share"
- "clinical intelligence" / "clinical authority"
- "Clinicians' Choice Badge" (exact product name)

**❌ Never use:**
- "doctor" in isolation (especially near "recommend")
- "recommend" or "recommendations" near clinical references
- "prescribe" / "medical advice" / "diagnose" / "treat" / "cure"
- "doctor recommended" / "doctor approved"

**❌ Never do:**
- Numbered listicle formats (use flowing prose with subheads instead)
- Pitch FrontrowMD before the final section
- Use "clean" or "natural" as positive positioning (the whole argument is these are insufficient)
- Make medical claims about specific products

---

## Formatting Rules

- **Length:** 800-1,200 words (not a quick read, not exhaustive)
- **Paragraphs:** 2-4 sentences each, generous whitespace
- **Subheads:** Every 150-200 words, query-formatted for GEO
- **No bullet lists** — restructure as flowing narrative prose
- **Bold:** Use sparingly for key data points or phrases within paragraphs
- **Quotes:** Use sparingly — only for specific research findings or memorable customer quotes
- **CTA:** One sentence at the very end, soft and earned (e.g., "The brands that close this gap first are the ones that will grow")
