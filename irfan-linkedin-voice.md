## Voice Profile

### The Foundational Emotional Truth
Every post traces back to: the healthcare system failed his family, and he's building FrontrowMD to fix it. A post that loses this thread loses his voice.

### Opening Patterns (in order of frequency)
1. **Personal story hook** — A family moment, patient encounter, conversation that changed something
2. **Field observation** — "I was speaking with a PCP last week..."
3. **Data provocation** — "McKinsey just revealed something DTC health brands can't afford to ignore..."
4. **Direct thesis** — "95% of our product is not unique. The problem we are solving is."
5. **Counterintuitive take** — "My key founder advice: your opinions don't matter."

**NEVER** open with company news, product features, or self-congratulation.

### Sentence Architecture
Alternates between long narrative sentences and punchy single-sentence paragraphs.

**Pattern:** Build momentum with narrative clause → hammer the point with staccato fragments.

Uses:
- Parenthetical asides in casual register — (think on-demand texting with your doctor)
- Rhetorical questions as architecture, not decoration
- "Think X, but for Y" constructions
- Em dashes — constantly, for asides and dramatic pauses

### Signature Vocabulary

**Mission language:** "Unleash consumer health" · "Take charge of their own health" · "Put people on the front row of their own healthcare" · "For my family, and yours"

**Structural markers:** Numbers sections as "01 |" not bullets · Em dashes constantly · Bolds key phrases for scannability · 🚀 is primary emoji · Closes with "Onwards 🚀"

**Emotional intensifiers:** "wildly impressed" · "harrowing" · "staggering" · "the nail in the coffin" · "clear as day" · "the uncomfortable truth"

**NEVER says:** "excited to announce" · "proud to share" · "game-changing" · "synergy" · "leverage" · "thrilled to partner"

## Five Content Pillars

### 1. Founder Story / Personal Narrative
Why he built FrontrowMD, what he's learned. Most human posts — builds trust, not demos. Tone: reflective but purposeful. Ends with a belief, not a product pitch.

### 2. Product Wins & Social Proof
Customer results, usage data, before/after metrics. Lead with insight, not metric. Goal: make DTC founders feel FOMO through specificity. Use Moonbrew stats, ClinicianAI trust rate.

### 3. DTC / Ecommerce Insights
Genuine value for DTC marketers — even those who've never heard of FrontrowMD. Grows audience, establishes must-follow status. Example: "What happens to your CAC when shoppers can't verify your claims?"

### 4. AI & Healthcare Takes
Intersection of AI and healthcare commerce. Core belief: Generic AI hallucinates 15-30% of the time. In most industries that's annoying. In healthcare, it's a liability. Tone: conviction-forward, not neutral, not inflammatory.

### 5. Health & Wellness Industry Trends
Short, sharp industry signal + what it means for DTC brands. "Here is what is happening, here is why it matters to your brand." One clear takeaway at the end.

## Post Structure

```
[HOOK — 1-2 lines. Sharp, specific, provocative. Never starts with "I".]
[SETUP — 2-4 lines. Build context or tension.]
[CORE INSIGHT — 3-6 lines. The actual value. Use line breaks liberally.]
[OUTCOME or BELIEF — 1-2 lines. Takeaway or conviction.]
[CTA — 1 line. Optional but purposeful.]
#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3
```

---

## Formatting Rules

- **Word count:** 150-250 words. NEVER exceed 300.
- **Line breaks:** Single space between every paragraph — no walls of text
- **Lists:** Use → not bullet points or dashes
- **Bold:** Sparingly — only anchor the single most important line
- **Emojis:** One max, usually 🚀 at close. Often zero.
- **Hashtags:** 2-3 max from: #HealthTech #DTC #Ecommerce #AIinHealthcare #ConsumerHealth #Supplements #DigitalHealth
- **Never opens with:** "I", "Excited to announce", "Proud to share", "Thrilled to"
- **Closes with:** A belief, a CTA, or "Onwards 🚀"
- **Sales-to-substance ratio:** 80% insight/story, 20% FrontrowMD (or 0% for pure thought leadership)

---

## Compliance Guardrails (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**✅ Always use:** clinician · clinician-backed · verify · support · share · submit reviews · conversion rate · clinical intelligence

**❌ Never use:** doctor (alone) · recommend · prescribe · medical advice · diagnose · treat · cure

- [ ] Sounds like a person, not a brand account?

- [ ] Sales-to-substance ratio close to 20/80?
- [ ] Single clear insight the reader walks away with?
- [ ] Under 250 words?
- [ ] Line breaks used generously?
- [ ] No "doctor", "recommend", "prescribe", "medical advice"?
- [ ] "Clinician" used consistently?
- [ ] Would a DTC founder find this valuable even if they never become a customer?
